About

Hi! I'm Roshan Pathak. I am an undergraduate Neuroscience student at Johns Hopkins University, and an undergraduate research assistant in Johns Hopkins Medical Institutions (JHMI). 

Besides my academic work, I am passionate about health equity and APIDA (Asian, Pacific Islander, Desi American) advocacy. You'll see my involvements with these causes scattered through my materials.

Besides that, I love art & media, and you'll find some of that on this page as well! 

academic

As a neuroscience student who is interested in entering medicine, my academic and research interests lie in neurobiology, the underlying molecular systems that govern neurological processes. Moreover, I am interested in how understanding these systems can impact medicine, rational drug design, and neurological treatments.

I am currently a newly aquired undergraduate research assistant in the 
Twomey Lab at Johns Hopkins Medical School in the Neuroscience and Biophysics Departments. The lab investigates the mechanism of assembly of AMPA receptors and the application of tight junctions to drug design by using Cryolectron microscopy.

experiences

Outside of my academic pursuits, I am a firm believer in the importance of civic engagement and service. As a result, I began my college experience taking up the role of Board Liaison for the Inter-Asian Council, an organization that attempts to uplift the voices of APIDA students. I've also supplemented this with my position as a mentor with the Charm City Science League, teaching science in Baltimore City Public Schools.

In the ongoing effort to combat health inequity worldwide, there are innovative solutions to be found. Working with my classmates to found a chapter of the organization Blueprints 4 Pangaea (B4P), I seek to reduce waste and improve equity in medicine. B4P partners with nonprofits and major hospitals to reallocate unused medical supplies, either to local clinics or overseas. Currently, I am in charge of social media, outreach, and marketing for our school's chapter.

Because of my intention to pursue medicine I am equally passionate about gaining experience in patient care settings. These settings include:


the Raritan Valley Regional EMS Squad, where I am an observer and volunteer.

the Garden State Physicians Office, where I am a volunteer medical assistant.

the Lenox Hill Neurosurgery BRAINterns program, where I virtually shadowed and took seminars with the Lenox Hill Hospital Neurosurgery Department.
